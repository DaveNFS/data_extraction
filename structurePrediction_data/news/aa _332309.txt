Colin Powell to Deliver Keynote Address at AMR Research's Supply Chain Executive

Conference;

Event will take Place at The Phoenician Resort, Scottsdale, AZ May 30-June 1,

2007



LENGTH: 428 words



DATELINE: BOSTON Feb. 27





BOSTON, Feb. 27  /PRNewswire-USNewswire/ -- Today, AMR Research announced that

former Secretary of State and Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff Colin Powell

will deliver the keynote address at the company's annual Supply Chain Executive

Conference.



Presentations and sessions will focus on the theme of handling strategic risk

and opportunity across a global supply chain.  In his keynote, Colin Powell will

draw on his experience as both a soldier and statesman in developing and

executing strategies for managing risk while seizing opportunity.



In addition to AMR Research analysts, executives scheduled to present at the

conference include:



  * Erik Autor, Vice President, Trade Counsel, National Retail Federation



   * Irial Finan, President, Bottling Investments and Supply Chain,

     The Coca-Cola Company



   * Anita Roper, Director Sustainability, Alcoa



   * Howard Smith, Global Vice President, Supply Chain Operations, Polo

     Ralph Lauren



   * Tom van Laar, Head Global Technical Operations, Novartis Pharma AG



   * Jim Waters, Vice President Caterpillar Production System Division,

     Caterpillar, Inc.



   * Don Weintritt, Director of Global Supply Chain, Dow Chemical



   Business and supply chain questions to be addressed at the conference

   include:



   * How does a global company plan for supply network risks in the rollout

     of new products?



   * What are the tradeoffs in doing business in developing economies? How

     can supply chain help businesses manage these tradeoffs best?



   * What are the implications for sustainability as global economic

     development accelerates?



   * What role does the retail sector play in globalization? How can retail

     supply chains respond to risk at the consumer level?
