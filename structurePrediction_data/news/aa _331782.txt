17th World Aluminium Conference: What are the Key Strategic Threats for

Aluminium Producers?



LENGTH: 324 words



DATELINE: ABU DHABI, U.A.E., April 12, 2012





Keynote presentation looking at "the key strategic threats for aluminium

producers" with Saeed Fadhel Al Mazrooei, President and CEO, EMAL, Jacynthe

Côté, CEO, Rio Tinto Alcan, Abdulla J.M. Kalban, President and CEO, DUBAL, Phil

Martens, President and CEO, Novelis Inc, Chris Ayers, EVP and President of

Global Primary Products, Alcoa



CRU's 17th World Aluminium Conference will take place from 30 April - 2 May 2012

during an event filled week for the aluminium industry in Abu Dhabi, which

includes the Gulf Aluminium Dinner and other important industry meetings. With

so many top executives in the region, and an expected audience of over 300, the

conference promises to be the definitive aluminium conference of the year.



The programme will decipher the avalanche of industry news and focus on what's

important for the aluminium sector. With a workshop about optimising your cast

house mix and many illuminating presentations on the economy, supply, demand,

energy and raw materials, attendees will benefit immensely by attending, both in

terms of the high value intellectual property available and in terms of

excellent networking.



Conference sponsors: EMAL, Alba, ABB, SNC-Lavalin, Ovivo, Mubadala, Samsung,

Alucor



CRU is an independent business analysis, and consultancy group focused on the

mining, metals and fertilizer sectors. Founded in the late 1960s and still

privately owned to ensure its independence, the group employs more than 200

experts in London, Beijing, Mumbai, Santiago, Sydney and key centres within the

United States.



CRU's events team produce international conferences on core industries and

sectors that provide high-level networking opportunities - attracting executive

level speakers which guarantee that delegates are well informed on the global

changes affecting their industries.
