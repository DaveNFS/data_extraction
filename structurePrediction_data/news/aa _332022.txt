Pittsburgh Council on Higher Education to Respond to Mayor's Student Tax

Proposal



LENGTH: 176 words



DATELINE: PITTSBURGH, Nov. 9





PITTSBURGH, Nov. 9 /PRNewswire/ -- Dr. Mary Hines, chair of the Pittsburgh

Council on Higher Education (PCHE), will meet with reporters at 10 a.m.,

Tuesday, November 10, to respond to Mayor Luke Ravenstahl's proposed tax on the

city's college and university students.



Dr. Hines, also president of Carlow University, will represent all the PCHE

members including Chatham University, Pittsburgh Theological Seminary, Carnegie

Mellon University, Robert Morris University, Duquesne University, Point Park

University, LaRoche College, Community College of Allegheny County, University

of Pittsburgh and Carlow University. Many of the university presidents will also

be in attendance.



The news conference will be held in the AE Hunt Room on the 23rd floor of the

Regional Enterprise Tower (the old Alcoa Building).
