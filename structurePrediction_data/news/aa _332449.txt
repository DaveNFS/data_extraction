FineGround Executive to Join Networld+Interop 2005 Panel on Enterprise

Application Performance



LENGTH: 432 words



DATELINE: CAMPBELL, Calif. April 26





CAMPBELL, Calif., April 26  /PRNewswire/ -- FineGround Inc., the leader in

application and file delivery across the extended enterprise, today announced

that Jay Mellman, FineGround's vice president of marketing, will be a featured

panelist at Networld+Interop Las Vegas 2005, the leading destination for

networking technology buyers and sellers.  FineGround will also be exhibiting at

the event in booth 559.



   WHAT:     The panel, sponsored by NetForecast and moderated by

              NetForecast President Peter Sevcik, will explore methods for

              monitoring, affecting and improving application performance

              over the Internet.  The typical business that operates over the

              Internet does not own, control, or sometimes even know the

              status of the switching fabric, servers, and end-stations upon

              which it relies.  In this session, entitled "Improving

              Performance Over the Internet -- It CAN be Done," FineGround

              will address strategies to help attendees understand and

              improve the performance of business-critical applications

              operating over the Internet.



    WHEN:     Thursday, May 5 at 11:30 a.m. PDT



    WHERE:    Networld+Interop Las Vegas 2005

              Conference Session Room 3

              Mandalay Bay Convention Center, Las Vegas, Nevada



    To learn more about Networld+Interop 2005, please visit the conference

    Web site at  http://www.interop.com/ .



   About FineGround





FineGround is the leading provider of network appliances that accelerate and

optimize application and file delivery across the extended enterprise. Through a

unique combination of application-specific techniques for bandwidth reduction,

latency management, protocol transformation and server offloading, FineGround's

data center solutions optimize the performance of interactive applications and

file systems across wide area networks (WANs).  FineGround's Velocity appliances

for enterprise application delivery (EAD) and wide area file services (WAFS) are

used by more than 100 of the world's largest global enterprises, including

Alcoa, BMW, Cargill, Royal Mail, Sun Microsystems, Walgreens and Whirlpool.

FineGround is a privately held, venture-backed company headquartered in

Campbell, California with regional headquarters in London and Hong Kong, and on

the Web at  http://www.fineground.com/ .
