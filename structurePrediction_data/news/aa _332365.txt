I.D. Systems' Second Quarter 2006 Earnings Conference Call



LENGTH: 351 words



DATELINE: HACKENSACK, N.J. July 20





HACKENSACK, N.J., July 20  /PRNewswire-FirstCall/ -- I.D. Systems, Inc.

(NASDAQ:IDSY) will be holding its quarterly financial results conference call

for investors and analysts on August 8, 2006 at 4:45 p.m. ET. The financial

results announcement is scheduled to cross the wire shortly after the market

close on August 8, 2006.  I.D. Systems, Inc. is sending out this release

regarding Q2-2006 to comply with Federal Regulation FD.



Financial analysts will be notified via fax/e-mail with a toll-free number to

access the Q2-2006 conference call.  Interested parties can also listen to a

live webcast at  http://www.id-systems.com/investor_relations.html . Any

questions should be directed in advance to  ir@id-systems.com .



The conference call will be archived at I.D. Systems' website for approximately

90 days.



The webcast is also being distributed through the Thomson StreetEvents Network

to both institutional and individual investors.  Individual investors can listen

to the call at  http://www.fulldisclosure.com/ , Thomson/CCBN's individual

investor portal, powered by StreetEvents.  Institutional investors can access

the call via Thomson's password-protected event management site, StreetEvents (

http://www.streetevents.com/ ).



About I.D. Systems



I.D. Systems provides proven wireless solutions for tracking and managing lift

trucks, cranes, fixed machinery, and other powered equipment.  The company's

Wireless Asset Net(TM) system improves safety and reduces fleet costs through

access control, location tracking, electronic safety checklists,

motion/speed/distance/weight/impact sensing, utilization monitoring, and much

more.  Customers include 3M Company, Alcoa, American Axle, Archer Daniels

Midland, DaimlerChrysler, Ford Motor Company, Northrop Grumman, Target

Corporation, Toyota, Walgreen Co., the U.S. Department of Homeland Security, the

U.S. Navy, and the U.S. Postal Service, among many others.
