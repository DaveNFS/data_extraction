Million Trees LA and ShareFest Partner for Project in Wilmington Along Pacific

Coast Highway



LENGTH: 428 words



DATELINE: LOS ANGELES April 24





LOS ANGELES, April 24 /PRNewswire/ -- ShareFest Community Development and

Million Trees LA announced today a joint project to plant 100 trees along a

stretch of Pacific Coast Highway from Figueroa Blvd. to Eubank Ave. in the local

business district of Wilmington. Million Trees LA is an initiative begun by

Mayor Antonio Villaraigosa's Office in 2006 to plants trees and transform the

City of Los Angeles into a green and sustainable city.



(Photo: http://www.newscom.com/cgi-bin/prnh/20080424/LATH118 )



The tree planting is one of more than 50 projects from Pico Union to Wilmington

and across the South Bay that will be a part of ShareFest's 5th Annual Workday

April 26th. Nearly 3,000 volunteers have signed up in advance to participate

through more than 90 different service organizations, churches, and schools.



In the Wilmington area, two additional projects will be taking place including

the painting of a mural in a local park at the corner of Watson Ave. and O

Street, and Wilmington Middle School, that serves more than 2,200 children from

the area.



ShareFest Community Development, Inc. is a non-profit public benefit

corporation. It is funded through donations, grants, volunteer hours, and

sponsorships.



Community Sponsors; Mayor Antonio R. Villaraigosa, City of Los Angeles, Port of

Los Angeles, BNSF. Neighborhood Sponsors; Dobruck Designs, Carnegie Marketing,

Water, Million Trees LA. Supporting Sponsor; Coastal Interactive, Cuningham

ShareFest's vision is to unite the South Bay and meet the deepest needs within

the communities of southwestern Los Angeles County. Cities within this area

include El Segundo, Manhattan Beach, Hawthorne, Lawndale, Gardena, Carson,

Hermosa Beach, Redondo Beach, Torrance, Harbor City, Lomita and the communities

of the Palos Verdes Peninsula. For more information on how to donate or

volunteer visit http://www.sharefestinc.org/ or call 310-626-8106.
